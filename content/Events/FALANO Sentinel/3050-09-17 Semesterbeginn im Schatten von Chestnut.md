## Winter Semester Begins with a New Threat on Campus

The winter semester has begun, and the universities and academies of the nation are filling up with students from all over the Republic. A lively hustle and bustle sets in as living spaces are changed and learning materials are acquired. But now, with a new component on the shopping list: Chestnut, Gummi, or Chleb.

"We have great cause for concern," explained Lois McRacher, Commander of the Law Enforcement and expert in addiction control. "The Triumvirate's campaign against Chestnut had a sluggish start but ultimately gave us many leads, and we were able to arrest over 371 people in connection with the drug. It, of course, remains difficult, given how it is distributed and produced in a network-like manner. So far, we can't say who is pushing the sale of the drug and ultimately making the big profit, but we do know one thing: the middlemen and sub-producers work on a kind of franchise principle and are also massively exploited."

Universities are now sounding the alarm, as the drug is in massive circulation among students. Walaux Academy alone has expelled 76 new students in the first week for possession, dealing, or consumption of drugs. The prevalent drug: Chestnut, in its usual wide variety of forms.

"At Walaux Academy, there is a zero-tolerance policy for drug use and distribution. This is neither necessary nor a good signal, we have to counteract it with a clear line," explained Zofia Kowalska, the Academy's Disciplinary Councillor, while smoking a cigarette. She stated that the Triumvirate's measure did not have the desired effect, or may have even contributed to Chestnut gaining more name recognition and interest. The academy has, however, requested addiction experts from the Republic to both clarify and support the ongoing investigation, to assess the extent of the problem, and then to initiate suitable measures for its containment.

We point out that the drug called "Chestnut," "Gummi," or "Chleb" only has a short-term positive effect on stress, anxieties, and performance levels. An addiction to "Chestnut" occurs after the first use. Therefore, to be safe, only buy products from shops on campus that have been legitimized by the university administration. Any information on suspicious behavior and the "weird smile" should be reported immediately to the university administration or the Law Enforcement office.
