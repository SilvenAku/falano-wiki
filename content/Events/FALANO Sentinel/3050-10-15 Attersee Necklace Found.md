## Sensational Find in the Heart of the VEU: 1,000-Year-Old Wooden Pendant Discovered

A sensational discovery has emerged from the heart of the VEU (Verband Europäischer Union, likely): A necklace over 1,000 years old has surfaced in a dilapidated house in the region known as the Salzkammergut. While this might not sound unusual, the pendant itself is made of wood.

At first glance, it looks like an island or a misshapen Spätzle (a type of soft egg noodle). However, it is actually an ancient depiction of **Lake Attersee**, whose shape has changed only minimally over the centuries.

"Through years of study, and utilizing our extensive expertise, we can now confirm that it is indeed the Attersee," stated **Renate Grauburgunder**, expert on the old Salzkammergut at Oida University Eurom. She noted that the immediate proximity of the discovery site to the lake played only a minor role in the analysis process.

The artifact is a **wooden pendant in the exact shape of Lake Attersee** dating back to the year 2000. It is approximately 5 cm long and 0.3 mm thick, hanging from a metal chain roughly 45 cm in length.

"A truly wonderful find that reflects our region so perfectly. We are connected to nature and stand for lasting values and quality that endure generations," explained **Gerhard Bauer**, Mayor of the municipality of St. Lenzing an der Ager.

The municipality intends to honor and spread this heritage by selling the rights for the reproduction of replicas of this sensational find for a small fee.
