"Nicht jedes geborene Kind ist gleichzeitig auch ein gewonnenes Leben im Port. Denn viele von ihnen wachsen unter grauenvollen Bedingungen auf. Die Regierung selbst hat wenig Macht darüber, wenn es darum geht Kinderexistenzen ohne elterliches Einvernehmen zu verbessern. ## At the Start of the Christmas Season: Focusing on "Children of Falano"

As is tradition, every year at the start of the Christmas season, we shine a special light on the numerous charitable organizations in the Port. This year, we are looking at the exceptional institution, Children of Falano.

"Not every child born in the Port is simultaneously a life won. Because many of them grow up under horrific conditions. The government itself has little power to improve the existence of children without parental consent. Therefore, it is all the more important that we, as an institution, are active, at least in nurseries, kindergartens, and schools," explained **Leeha Ji'hn**, spokesperson for the NGO Children of Falano.

She elaborated that they specifically "not only ask if something is wrong, but—since often the children don't even know that what is happening to them or not happening to them is wrong—they look to see if something is missing."

Last year, Children of Falano helped over 45,000 children. Their program ranges from providing new accommodation, foster parents, tutoring for school, support in social skills, and providing food and restorative leisure time. This is because people often forget how important routines and procedures are in giving life stability, even if we often perceive them as a burden.
